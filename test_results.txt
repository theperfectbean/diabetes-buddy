TEST: Basal rate adjustment: 'What are best practices for adjusting basal insulin rates?'
======================================================================
Basal insulin is a fundamental part of diabetes management, providing a constant, low level of insulin to regulate blood glucose between mealtimes and overnight to support basic metabolism. This can be achieved through intermediate-acting or long-acting insulin analogs, or via continuous infusion of rapid-acting insulin using an insulin pump.

Here are some practical strategies to discuss with your healthcare team regarding basal insulin rate adjustments:

1.  **Basal Rate Determination for Pump Therapy:** If considering or using an insulin pump, it's crucial to discuss basal rate determination with your diabetes care professional before beginning pump therapy.
2.  **Utilizing Temporary Basal Rates:** For situations where insulin needs change temporarily, such as during periods of inactivity (e.g., a long car drive), discuss with your healthcare team how to use temporary basal rates to immediately increase or decrease your basal insulin delivery as needed.
3.  **Addressing Overnight Patterns:** Given that a dawn phenomenon pattern was detected in your personal data, consider discussing overnight basal adjustments or timing strategies with your healthcare team to help manage blood glucose levels during this period.
4.  **Consistent Administration of Long-Acting Basal Insulin:** If you use long-acting insulins for your basal component, discuss with your healthcare team the importance of administering them at the same time every day for consistent action, although they can be given at any time of day.
